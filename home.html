FAQs

What should I feed my pet?
The diet for your pet depends on their age, breed, and health condition. Generally, pets should be fed high-quality, age-appropriate food and given fresh water daily. Consult your veterinarian for personalized recommendations.
How often should I take my pet to the vet?
Pets should have regular veterinary check-ups at least once a year. However, puppies, kittens, and senior pets may require more frequent visits. Regular vet visits help in early detection of health issues and maintaining overall wellness.
How can I keep my pet's teeth healthy?
Regular brushing with pet-specific toothpaste, providing dental chews, and scheduling professional cleanings with your vet can help maintain your pet's dental health. Good dental hygiene prevents plaque build-up and oral diseases.
What are the signs of a sick pet?
Signs of illness in pets include changes in appetite, lethargy, vomiting, diarrhea, coughing, or changes in behavior. If you notice any of these signs, it's important to consult with your veterinarian as soon as possible.
